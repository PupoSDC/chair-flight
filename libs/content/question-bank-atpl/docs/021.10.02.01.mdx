# Types, grades, characteristics, limitations

## Learning Objectives

1.  **021.10.02.01.01** -- Name the type of fuel used for petrol engines
    including its colour (AVGAS); 100 (green); 100LL (blue).
2.  **021.10.02.01.02** -- Name the type of fuel normally used for aviation
    diesel engines (JET-A1..
3.  **021.10.02.01.03** -- Define the term "octane rating".
4.  **021.10.02.01.04** -- Define the term "detonation" and describe the causes
    and effects of detonation for both petrol and diesel engines.
5.  **021.10.02.01.05** -- Define the term "pre-ignition" and describe the
    causes and effects of pre-ignition for both petrol and diesel engines.
6.  **021.10.02.01.06** -- Identify the conditions and power settings that
    promote detonation for petrol engines.
7.  **021.10.02.01.07** -- Describe how detonation in petrol engines is
    recognised.
8.  **021.10.02.01.08** -- Describe the method and occasions for checking the
    fuel for water content.
9.  **021.10.02.01.09** -- State the typical value of fuel density for aviation
    gasoline and diesel fuel.
10. **021.10.02.01.10** -- Explain volatility, viscosity and vapour locking for
    petrol and diesel fuels.
