# General criteria

## Learning Objectives

1.  **010.06.04.01.01** -- State the general criteria (except "Speeds for
    procedure calculations") of the approach procedure design: instrument
    approach areas; accuracy of fixes; fixes formed by intersections;
    intersection fix-tolerance factors; other fix-tolerance factors; descent
    gradient.
2.  **010.06.04.01.02** -- Name the five possible segments of an instrument
    approach procedure.
3.  **010.06.04.01.03** -- State the reasons for establishing aircraft
    categories for the approach.
4.  **010.06.04.01.04** -- State the maximum angle between the final approach
    track and the extended RWY centre line to still consider a non-precision
    approach as being a "straight-in approach".
5.  **010.06.04.01.05** -- State the minimum obstacle clearance (MOC) provided
    by the minimum sector altitudes (MSAs) established for an aerodrome.
6.  **010.06.04.01.06** -- State that a pilot shall apply wind corrections when
    carrying out an instrument approach procedure.
7.  **010.06.04.01.07** -- State the most significant factor influencing the
    conduct of instrument approach procedures.
8.  **010.06.04.01.08** -- Explain why a pilot should not descend below obstacle
    clearance altitude/height (OCA/H), which are established for: precision
    approach procedures; non-precision approach procedures; visual (circling)
    procedures; APV approach procedures.
9.  **010.06.04.01.09** -- Describe in general terms the relevant factors for
    the calculation of operational minima.
10. **010.06.04.01.10** -- State the following acronyms in plain language: DA,
    DH, OCA, OCH, MDA, MDH, MOC, DA/H, OCA/H, MDA/H.
11. **010.06.04.01.11** -- Explain the relationship between the terms: DA, DH,
    OCA, OCH, MDA, MDH, MOC, DA/H, OCA/H, and MDA/H.
