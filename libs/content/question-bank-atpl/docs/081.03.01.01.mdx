# Flow separation at increasing alpha

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.03.01.01.01** -- Define the "boundary layer".
2.  **081.03.01.01.02** -- Describe the thickness of a typical laminar and
    turbulent boundary layer.
3.  **081.03.01.01.03** -- Describe the properties, advantages and disadvantages
    of the laminar boundary layer.
4.  **081.03.01.01.04** -- Describe the properties, advantages and disadvantages
    of the turbulent boundary layer.
5.  **081.03.01.01.05** -- Define the "transition point".
6.  **081.03.01.01.06** -- Explain why the laminar boundary layer separates
    easier than the turbulent boundary layer does.
7.  **081.03.01.01.07** -- Describe why the airflow over the aft part of a wing
    slows down as the alpha increases.
8.  **081.03.01.01.08** -- Define the "separation point" and describe its
    location as a function of alpha.
9.  **081.03.01.01.09** -- Define alphaCRIT.
10. **081.03.01.01.10** -- Describe in straight and level flight the influence
    of increasing the alpha and the phenomenon that may occur regarding: the
    forward stagnation point; the pressure distribution; the CP location
    (straight and swept-back wing); CL; CD and D (drag); the pitching moment
    (straight and swept-back wing); buffet onset; deterrent buffet for a clean
    wing at high Mach number; lack of pitch authority; uncommanded pitch down;
    uncommanded roll.
11. **081.03.01.01.11** -- Explain what causes the possible natural buffet on
    the aeroplane in a pre-stall condition.
12. **081.03.01.01.12** -- Describe the effectiveness of the flight controls in
    a pre-stall condition.
13. **081.03.01.01.13** -- Describe and explain the normal post-stall behaviour
    of a straight-wing aeroplane.
14. **081.03.01.01.14** -- Describe the effect and dangers of using the controls
    close to the stall.
15. **081.03.01.01.15** -- Describe the deterrent buffet.
16. **081.03.01.01.16** -- Explain the occurrence of the deterrent buffet and
    why this phenomenon is considered to be a stall limit.
