# Special phenomena of stall

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.03.01.05.01** -- Intentionally left blank
2.  **081.03.01.05.02** -- Explain the difference between power-off and power-on
    stalls and recovery.
3.  **081.03.01.05.03** -- Describe stall and recovery in a climbing and
    descending turn.
4.  **081.03.01.05.04** -- Describe the pitch-up effect on a swept wing
    aeroplane and also an aeroplane with a T-tail.
5.  **081.03.01.05.05** -- Describe super stall or deep stall.
6.  **081.03.01.05.06** -- Describe the philosophy behind the stick-pusher
    system.
7.  **081.03.01.05.07** -- Describe the factors that can lead to the absence of
    stall warning and explain the associated risks.
8.  **081.03.01.05.08** -- Describe the indications and explain the consequences
    of premature stabiliser stall due to ice contamination (negative tail
    stall).
9.  **081.03.01.05.09** -- Describe when to expect in-flight icing.
10. **081.03.01.05.10** -- Explain how the effect is changed when
    retracting/extending lift-augmentation devices.
11. **081.03.01.05.11** -- Intentionally left blank
12. **081.03.01.05.12** -- Explain the effect of a contaminated wing on the
    stall speed and alphaCRIT.
13. **081.03.01.05.13** -- Explain the hazards associated with airframe
    contamination when parked and during ground operations in winter conditions,
    and the aerodynamic effects when attempting a take-off.
14. **081.03.01.05.14** -- Explain de-icing/anti-icing holdover time and the
    likely hazards after it has expired.
15. **081.03.01.05.15** -- Describe the aerodynamic effects of heavy tropical
    rain on stall speed and drag, and the appropriate mitigation in such
    conditions.
