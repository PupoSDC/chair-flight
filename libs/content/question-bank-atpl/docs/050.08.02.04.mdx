# Monsoon, sandstorms, cold-air outbreaks

## Learning Objectives

1.  **050.08.02.04.01** -- Define in general the term "monsoon" and give a
    general overview of regions of occurrence.
2.  **050.08.02.04.02** -- Describe the major monsoon conditions. (Refer to
    Subject 050 08 02 02.
3.  **050.08.02.04.03** -- Explain how trade winds change character after a long
    track and become monsoon winds.
4.  **050.08.02.04.04** -- Explain the weather and the flight hazards associated
    with a monsoon.
5.  **050.08.02.04.05** -- Explain the formation of the SW/NE monsoon over West
    Africa and describe the weather, stressing the seasonal differences.
6.  **050.08.02.04.06** -- Explain the formation of the SW/NE monsoon over India
    and describe the weather, stressing the seasonal differences.
7.  **050.08.02.04.07** -- Explain the formation of the monsoon over the Far
    East and northern Australia and describe the weather, stressing the seasonal
    differences.
8.  **050.08.02.04.08** -- Describe the formation and properties of sandstorms.
9.  **050.08.02.04.09** -- Indicate when and where outbreaks of cold polar air
    can enter subtropical weather systems.
10. **050.08.02.04.10** -- Name well-known examples of polar-air outbreaks
    (Blizzard, Pampero).
