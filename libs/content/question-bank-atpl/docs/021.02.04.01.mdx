# Construction, functions, loads

## Learning Objectives

1.  **021.02.04.01.01** -- Describe the following types of fuselage
    construction:
    - monocoque,
    - semi-monocoque.
2.  **021.02.04.01.02** -- Describe the construction and the function of the
    following structural components of a fuselage:
    - frames;
    - bulkhead;
    - pressure bulkhead;
    - stiffeners, stringers, longerons;
    - skin, doublers;
    - floor suspension (crossbeams);
    - floor panels;
    - firewall.
3.  **021.02.04.01.03** -- Describe the loads on the fuselage due to
    pressurisation.
4.  **021.02.04.01.04** -- Describe the following loads on a main landing gear:
    touch-down loads (vertical and horizontal); taxi loads on bogie gear
    (turns).
5.  **021.02.04.01.05** -- Describe the structural danger of a nose-wheel
    landing with respect to: fuselage loads; nose-wheel strut loads.
6.  **021.02.04.01.06** -- Describe the structural danger of a tail strike with
    respect to: fuselage and aft bulkhead damage (pressurisation).
7.  **021.02.04.01.07** -- Describe the door and hatch construction for
    pressurised and unpressurised aeroplanes including: door and frame (plug
    type); hinge location; locking mechanism.
8.  **021.02.04.01.08** -- Explain the advantages and disadvantages of the
    following fuselage cross sections: circular; double bubble; oval;
    rectangular.
9.  **021.02.04.01.09** -- Explain why flight-deck windows are constructed with
    different layers.
10. **021.02.04.01.10** -- Explain the function of window heating for structural
    purposes.
11. **021.02.04.01.11** -- Explain the implication of a direct-vision window
    (see CS 25.773(b)(3.).
12. **021.02.04.01.12** -- Explain the need for an eye-reference position.
13. **021.02.04.01.13** -- Explain the function of floor venting (blow-out
    panels).
14. **021.02.04.01.14** -- Describe the construction and fitting of sliding
    doors.
