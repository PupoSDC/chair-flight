# General

## Learning Objectives

1.  **021.09.04.01.01** -- Explain the function of a busbar.
2.  **021.09.04.01.02** -- Describe the function of the following buses: AC bus;
    DC bus; emergency AC or DC bus; essential AC or DC bus; battery bus; hot
    bus, ground servicing or maintenance bus.
3.  **021.09.04.01.03** -- State that the aircraft structure can be used as a
    part of the electrical circuit (common earth) and explain the implications
    for electrical bonding.
4.  **021.09.04.01.04** -- Explain the function of external power.
5.  **021.09.04.01.05** -- State that a priority sequence exists between the
    different sources of electrical power on ground and in flight.
6.  **021.09.04.01.06** -- Explain the term "load sharing".
7.  **021.09.04.01.07** -- Explain the term "load shedding".
8.  **021.09.04.01.08** -- Describe typical systems that can be shed in the
    event of a supply failure, such as passenger entertainment system and galley
    power.
9.  **021.09.04.01.09** -- Interpret a typical electrical system schematic to
    the level of detail as found in an aircraft FCOM.
10. **021.09.04.01.10** -- Explain the difference between a supply (e.g.
    generator) failure and a bus failure, and the operating consequences of
    either.
