# Airflow through the rotor disc and around the blades

## Learning Objectives

1.  **082.04.01.01.01** -- Based on Newton"s second law (momentum), explain that
    the upward vertical force from the disc, i.e. the rotor thrust, is the
    result of vertical downward velocities inside the rotor disc.
2.  **082.04.01.01.02** -- Explain why the production of the induced flow
    requires power applied to the shaft, i.e. induced power. Induced power is
    least if the induced velocities have the same value on the whole disc (i.e.
    there is uniformity of flow over the disc).
3.  **082.04.01.01.03** -- Explain why vertical rotor thrust must be higher than
    the weight of the helicopter because of the vertical drag on the fuselage.
4.  **082.04.01.01.04** -- Define the pitch angle and the alpha of a blade
    element.
5.  **082.04.01.01.05** -- Explain L and D relating to a blade element
    (including induced and profile drag).
6.  **082.04.01.01.06** -- Explain the necessity for collective pitch angle
    changes, the influence on the alpha and rotor thrust, and the need for blade
    feathering.
7.  **082.04.01.01.07** -- Describe the different blade shapes (as viewed from
    above).
8.  **082.04.01.01.08** -- Explain how profile drag on the blade elements
    generates a torque on the main shaft, and define the resulting rotor profile
    power.
9.  **082.04.01.01.09** -- Explain the influence of air density on the required
    powers.
