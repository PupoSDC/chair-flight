# Basic concepts of airflow

## Learning Objectives

1.  **082.01.01.04.01** -- Describe steady and unsteady airflow.
2.  **082.01.01.04.02** -- Define "streamline" and "stream tube".
3.  **082.01.01.04.03** -- Explain the principle of the continuity equation or
    the conservation of mass.
4.  **082.01.01.04.04** -- Describe the mass flow rate through a stream tube
    section.
5.  **082.01.01.04.05** -- State Bernoulli"s equation and use it to explain and
    define the relationship between static, dynamic and total pressure.
6.  **082.01.01.04.06** -- Define the stagnation point in the flow around an
    aerofoil, and explain the pressure obtained at the stagnation point.
7.  **082.01.01.04.07** -- Use the pitot system to explain the measurement of
    airspeed (no compressibility effects).
8.  **082.01.01.04.08** -- Define "TAS", "IAS", and "CAS".
9.  **082.01.01.04.09** -- Define two-dimensional airflow and its relationship
    to an aerofoil of infinite span (i.e. no blade tip vortices and, therefore,
    no induced drag). Explain the difference between two- and three-dimensional
    airflows.
10. **082.01.01.04.10** -- Explain that viscosity is a feature of any fluid (gas
    or liquid).
11. **082.01.01.04.11** -- Explain the tangential friction between air and the
    surface of an aerofoil, and the development of a boundary layer.
12. **082.01.01.04.12** -- Describe laminar and turbulent boundary layers and
    the transition from laminar to turbulent. Show the influence of the
    roughness of the surface on the position of the transition point.
