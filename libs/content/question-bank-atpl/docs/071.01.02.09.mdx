# Flight crew

## Learning Objectives

1.  **071.01.02.09.01** -- Explain the requirement regarding flight crew
    composition and in-flight relief.
2.  **071.01.02.09.02** -- Explain the requirement for conversion training and
    checking.
3.  **071.01.02.09.03** -- Explain the requirement for differences training and
    familiarisation training.
4.  **071.01.02.09.04** -- Explain the conditions for upgrade from co-pilot to
    commander.
5.  **071.01.02.09.05** -- Explain the minimum qualification requirements to
    operate as a commander.
6.  **071.01.02.09.06** -- Explain the requirement for recurrent training and
    checking.
7.  **071.01.02.09.07** -- Explain the requirement for a pilot to operate on
    either pilot"s seat.
8.  **071.01.02.09.08** -- Explain the minimum recent experience requirements
    for the commander and the co-pilot.
9.  **071.01.02.09.09** -- Specify the route and aerodrome/heliport knowledge
    required for a PIC/commander.
10. **071.01.02.09.10** -- Explain the requirement to operate on more than one
    aircraft type or variant.
11. **071.01.02.09.11** -- Explain that when a flight crew member operates both
    helicopters and aeroplanes, the operations are limited to one of each type.
12. **071.01.02.09.12** -- Explain the requirement(s) for training records.
13. **071.01.02.09.13** -- Explain the crew members" responsibilities in the
    execution of their duties, and define the commander"s authority.
14. **071.01.02.09.14** -- Explain the operator"s and commander"s
    responsibilities regarding persons on board, admission to the flight crew
    compartment and carriage of unauthorised persons or cargo.
15. **071.01.02.09.15** -- Explain the requirements for the initial operator"s
    crew resource management (CRM) training.
