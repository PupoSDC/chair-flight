# The stall speed

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.03.01.02.01** -- Explain VS0, VS1, VSR, and VS1G.
2.  **081.03.01.02.02** -- Solve VS1G from the lift formula given varying CL.
3.  **081.03.01.02.03** -- Describe and explain the influence of the following
    parameters on stall speed: CG; thrust component; slipstream; wing loading;
    mass; wing contamination; angle of sweep; altitude (for compressibility
    effects, see 081 02 03 02..
4.  **081.03.01.02.04** -- Define the "load factor n".
5.  **081.03.01.02.05** -- Explain why the load factor increases in a turn.
6.  **081.03.01.02.06** -- Explain why the load factor increases in a pull-up
    and decreases in a push-over manoeuvre.
7.  **081.03.01.02.07** -- Describe and explain the influence of the "load
    factor n" on stall speed.
8.  **081.03.01.02.08** -- Explain the expression "accelerated stall". Remark:
    Sometimes, accelerated stall is also erroneously referred to as high-speed
    stall. This latter expression will not be used for Subject 081.
9.  **081.03.01.02.09** -- Calculate the change of stall speed as a function of
    the load factor.
10. **081.03.01.02.10** -- Calculate the increase of stall speed in a horizontal
    coordinated turn as a function of bank angle.
11. **081.03.01.02.11** -- Calculate the change of stall speed as a function of
    the gross mass.
