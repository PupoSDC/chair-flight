# Cruise altitudes

## Learning Objectives

1.  **032.04.03.06.01** -- Define the term "optimum cruise altitude".
2.  **032.04.03.06.02** -- Explain the factors that affect optimum cruise
    altitude.
3.  **032.04.03.06.03** -- Explain the factors that can affect or limit the
    maximum operating cruise altitude.
4.  **032.04.03.06.04** -- Explain the purpose of, and operational reasons for,
    a step climb and when such a climb would be initiated for optimum range.
5.  **032.04.03.06.05** -- Describe the buffet onset boundary (BOB) and
    determine the high- and low-speed buffet (speed/Mach number only).
6.  **032.04.03.06.06** -- Analyse the influence of bank angle, mass and the
    1.3g buffet margin on a step climb.
7.  **032.04.03.06.07** -- Describe that the high-speed buffet can occur at
    speeds slower or faster than MMO.
8.  **032.04.03.06.08** -- Explain the reasons why a step climb may not be used
    (e.g. for short sectors, advantageous winds, avoiding turbulence, and due to
    air traffic restrictions).
