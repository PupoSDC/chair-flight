# Completion of a manual navigation plan

## Learning Objectives

1.  **033.02.01.07.01** -- Complete a navigation plan with the courses,
    distances and frequencies taken from charts.
2.  **033.02.01.07.02** -- Find the SID and STAR routes to be flown or to be
    expected.
3.  **033.02.01.07.03** -- Determine the position of top of climb (TOC) and top
    of descent (TOD) from given appropriate data.
4.  **033.02.01.07.04** -- Determine variation and calculate magnetic/true
    courses.
5.  **033.02.01.07.05** -- Calculate TAS from given aircraft performance data,
    altitude and OAT.
6.  **033.02.01.07.06** -- Calculate wind correction angles (WCAs)/drift and
    ground speeds (GSs).
7.  **033.02.01.07.07** -- Calculate individual and accumulated times for each
    leg to destination and alternate aerodromes.
8.  **033.02.01.07.08** -- Describe the advantages of global navigation
    satellite system/flight management computer (GNSS/FMC) equipment regarding:
    automatic calculation and display of tracks and leg distances; additional
    route information in the database (minimum altitudes, approach procedures);
    time and fuel estimates over waypoints; ability to adjust speed to arrive
    over a waypoint at a defined time; time and fuel revisions based on
    predicted and actual wind.
9.  **033.02.01.07.09** -- Describe the limitations of using GNSS/FMC equipment:
    pilot-inputted errors (flight levels, wind, temperature, fuel); the effect
    of other than predicted wind on fuel and time estimates; the effect of
    aircraft ºs non-standard configuration on flight management system (FMS)
    predictions.
