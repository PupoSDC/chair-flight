# Error generation

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.03.02.04.01** -- Distinguish between internal and external factors in
    error generation.
2.  **040.03.02.04.02** -- Identify possible sources of internal error
    generation.
3.  **040.03.02.04.03** -- Define and discuss the two errors associated with
    motor programmes (action slip and environmental capture).
4.  **040.03.02.04.04** -- List the three main sources of external error
    generation in the flight crew compartment.
5.  **040.03.02.04.05** -- Give examples to illustrate the following factors in
    external error generation in the flight crew compartment: ergonomics;
    economics; social environment.
6.  **040.03.02.04.06** -- Name the major goals in the design of human-centred
    human-machine interfaces.
7.  **040.03.02.04.07** -- Define the term "error tolerance".
8.  **040.03.02.04.08** -- List and describe the strategies that are used to
    reduce human error.
9.  **040.03.02.04.09** -- Describe the advantage of planning and the
    anticipation of future actions.
