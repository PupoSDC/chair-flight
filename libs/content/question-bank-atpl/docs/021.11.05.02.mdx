# Helicopter engine ratings, engine performance and limitations, engine handling: torque, performance aspects and limitations

## Learning Objectives

1.  **021.11.05.02.01** -- Describe engine rating torque limits for take-off,
    transient and maximum continuous.
2.  **021.11.05.02.02** -- Describe turbine outlet temperature (TOT) limits for
    take-off.
3.  **021.11.05.02.03** -- Explain why TOT is a limiting factor for helicopter
    performance.
4.  **021.11.05.02.04** -- Describe and explain the relationship between maximum
    torque available and density altitude, which leads to decreasing torque
    available with the increase of density altitude.
5.  **021.11.05.02.05** -- Explain that hovering downwind, on some helicopters,
    will noticeably increase the engine TOT.
6.  **021.11.05.02.06** -- Explain the reason why the engine performance is less
    when aircraft accessories (i.e. anti-ice, heating, hoist, filters) are
    switched on.
7.  **021.11.05.02.07** -- Describe the effects of use of bleed air on engine
    parameters.
8.  **021.11.05.02.08** -- Explain that, on some helicopters, exceeding the TOT
    limit may cause the main rotor to droop (slow down).
9.  **021.11.05.02.09** -- Describe overtorquing and explain the consequences.
