# Basic requirements and procedures

## Learning Objectives

1.  **010.06.06.01.01** -- Describe the two main objectives of altimeter
    settings.
2.  **010.06.06.01.02** -- Define the terms "QNH" and "QFE".
3.  **010.06.06.01.03** -- Describe the different terms for ALT or flight levels
    (FLs) respectively, which are the references during climb or descent to
    change the altimeter settings from QNH to 1013.2 hPa and vice versa.
4.  **010.06.06.01.04** -- Define the term "flight level (FL)".
5.  **010.06.06.01.05** -- State where FL zero shall be located.
6.  **010.06.06.01.06** -- State the interval by which consecutive FLs shall be
    separated.
7.  **010.06.06.01.07** -- Describe how FLs are defined.
8.  **010.06.06.01.08** -- Define the term "transition altitude (TA)".
9.  **010.06.06.01.09** -- State how TAs shall normally be specified.
10. **010.06.06.01.10** -- Explain how the HGT of the TA is calculated and
    expressed in practice.
11. **010.06.06.01.11** -- State where TAs shall be published.
12. **010.06.06.01.12** -- Define the term "transition level (TRL)".
13. **010.06.06.01.13** -- State when the TRL is normally passed on to the
    aircraft.
14. **010.06.06.01.14** -- State how the vertical position of the aircraft shall
    be expressed at or below the TA and TRL.
15. **010.06.06.01.15** -- Define the term "transition layer".
16. **010.06.06.01.16** -- Describe when the vertical position of an aircraft
    passing through the transition layer shall be expressed in terms of FLs and
    when in terms of ALT.
17. **010.06.06.01.17** -- State when the QNH altimeter setting shall be made
    available to departing aircraft.
18. **010.06.06.01.18** -- Explain when the vertical separation of an aircraft
    during en-route flight shall be assessed in terms of ALT and when in terms
    of FLs.
19. **010.06.06.01.19** -- Explain when, in air-ground communications during an
    en-route flight, the vertical position of an aircraft shall be expressed in
    terms of ALT and when in terms of FLs.
20. **010.06.06.01.20** -- Describe why QNH altimeter-setting reports should be
    provided from sufficient locations.
21. **010.06.06.01.21** -- State how a QNH altimeter setting shall be made
    available to aircraft approaching a controlled aerodrome (AD) for landing.
22. **010.06.06.01.22** -- State under which circumstances the vertical position
    of an aircraft above the TRL may be referenced in ALT.
