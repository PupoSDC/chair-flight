# Problem areas for pilots

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.02.03.03.01** -- State the role of the Eustachian tube in equalising
    pressure between the middle ear and the environment.
2.  **040.02.03.03.02** -- State that the in-flight environment may increase the
    severity of symptoms which may be minor while on the ground.
3.  **040.02.03.03.03** -- List the negative effects of suffering from colds or
    flu on flight operations especially with regard to the middle ear, the
    sinuses, and the teeth.
4.  **040.02.03.03.04** -- State when a pilot should seek medical advice from an
    aeromedical examiner (AME) or aeromedical centre (AeMC).
5.  **040.02.03.03.05** -- Describe the measures to prevent or clear problems
    due to pressure changes during flight.
6.  **040.02.03.03.06** -- Define "barotrauma".
7.  **040.02.03.03.07** -- Differentiate between otic, sinus, gastrointestinal
    and aerodontalgia (of the teeth) barotraumas and explain avoidance
    strategies.
8.  **040.02.03.03.08** -- Explain why the effects of otic barotrauma can be
    worse in the descent.
9.  **040.02.03.03.09** -- State the effects of gastrointestinal upsets that may
    occur during flight.
10. **040.02.03.03.10** -- List the precautions that should be observed to
    reduce the occurrence of gastrointestinal upsets.
11. **040.02.03.03.11** -- Indicate the major sources of gastrointestinal
    upsets.
12. **040.02.03.03.12** -- Define "obesity".
13. **040.02.03.03.13** -- State the following harmful effects obesity can
    cause: possibility of developing coronary problems; increased chances of
    developing diabetes; reduced ability to withstand G-forces; development of
    problems with the joints of the limbs; general circulatory problems; reduced
    ability to cope with hypoxia or decompression sickness; sleep apnoea.
14. **040.02.03.03.14** -- Describe the problems associated with Type 2 (mostly
    adult) diabetes: risk factors; insulin resistance; complications (vascular,
    neurological) and the consequences for the medical licence; pilots are not
    protected from Type 2 diabetes more than other people.
15. **040.02.03.03.15** -- Describe the typical back problems (unspecific back
    pain, slipped disc) that pilots have. Explain also the ways of preventing
    and treating these problems: good sitting posture; lumbar support; good
    physical condition; in-flight exercise, if possible; physiotherapy.
16. **040.02.03.03.16** -- Stress the importance of and methods to be adopted by
    aircrew, especially when travelling abroad, to avoid contaminated food and
    liquids.
17. **040.02.03.03.17** -- List the major contaminating sources in foodstuffs.
18. **040.02.03.03.18** -- State the major constituents of a healthy diet.
19. **040.02.03.03.19** -- State the measure to avoid hypoglycaemia.
20. **040.02.03.03.20** -- State the importance of adequate hydration.
21. **040.02.03.03.21** -- List the problems associated with operating in
    tropical climates.
22. **040.02.03.03.22** -- State the possible causes/sources of incapacitation
    in tropical countries with reference to: standards of hygiene; quality of
    water supply; insect-borne diseases; parasitic worms; rabies or other
    diseases that may be spread through contact with animals; sexually
    transmitted diseases.
23. **040.02.03.03.23** -- State the precautions to be taken to reduce the risks
    of developing problems in tropical areas.
24. **040.02.03.03.24** -- State the major infectious diseases that may severely
    incapacitate or kill individuals.
25. **040.02.03.03.25** -- State the precautions that must be taken to ensure
    that disease-carrying insects are not transported between areas.
