# Intoxication

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.02.03.04.01** -- State the harmful effects of tobacco on: the
    respiratory system; the cardiovascular system; the ability to resist
    hypoxia; the ability to withstand G-forces; night vision.
2.  **040.02.03.04.02** -- Indicate the level of caffeine dosage at which
    performance is degraded.
3.  **040.02.03.04.03** -- Besides coffee, indicate other beverages containing
    caffeine.
4.  **040.02.03.04.04** -- State the maximum acceptable limit of alcohol for
    flight crew according to the applicable regulations.
5.  **040.02.03.04.05** -- State the effects of alcohol consumption on: the
    ability to reason; inhibitions and self-control; vision; the sense of
    balance and sensory illusions; sleep patterns; hypoxia.
6.  **040.02.03.04.06** -- State the effects alcohol may have if consumed
    together with other drugs.
7.  **040.02.03.04.07** -- List the signs and symptoms of alcoholism.
8.  **040.02.03.04.08** -- List the factors that may be associated with the
    development of alcoholism.
9.  **040.02.03.04.09** -- Define the "unit" of alcohol and state the
    approximate elimination rate from the blood.
10. **040.02.03.04.10** -- State the maximum daily and weekly intake of units of
    alcohol which may be consumed without causing damage to the organs and
    systems of the human body.
11. **040.02.03.04.11** -- Discuss the actions that might be taken if a crew
    member is suspected of being an alcoholic.
12. **040.02.03.04.12** -- State the dangers associated with the use of
    non-prescription drugs.
13. **040.02.03.04.13** -- State the side effects of common non-prescription
    drugs used to treat colds, flu, hay fever and other allergies, especially
    medicines containing antihistamine preparations.
14. **040.02.03.04.14** -- Interpret the rules relevant to using (prescription
    or non-prescription) drugs that the pilot has not used before.
15. **040.02.03.04.15** -- Interpret the general rule that "if a pilot is so
    unwell that they require any medication, then they should consider
    themselves unfit to fly".
16. **040.02.03.04.16** -- List those materials present in an aircraft which
    may, when uncontained, cause severe health problems.
17. **040.02.03.04.17** -- List those aircraft-component parts which if burnt
    may give off toxic fumes.
18. **040.02.03.04.18** -- Describe a fume event and the possible incapacitating
    effects on those exposed to it.
