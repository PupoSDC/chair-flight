# Ailerons

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.05.04.01.01** -- Explain the functioning of ailerons.
2.  **081.05.04.01.02** -- Describe the adverse effects of aileron deflection.
    (Refer to Subjects 081 05 04 04 and 081 06 01 02.
3.  **081.05.04.01.03** -- Explain why some aeroplanes have inboard and outboard
    ailerons.
4.  **081.05.04.01.04** -- State that the outboard ailerons are locked beyond a
    given speed to prevent: over-control; exceeding structural limitations;
    aeroelastic phenomena (flutter, divergence and aileron reversal).
5.  **081.05.04.01.05** -- Describe the use of aileron deflection in normal
    flight, flight with sideslip, crosswind landings, horizontal turns, flight
    with one-engine-inoperative.
6.  **081.05.04.01.06** -- Define "roll rate".
7.  **081.05.04.01.07** -- List the factors that affect roll rate.
8.  **081.05.04.01.08** -- Describe flaperons and aileron droop.
