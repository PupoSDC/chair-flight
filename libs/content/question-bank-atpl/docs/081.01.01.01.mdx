# Laws and definitions

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.01.01.01.01** -- List the international system of units of measurement
    (SI) for mass, acceleration, weight, velocity, energy, density, temperature,
    pressure, force, wing loading, and power.
2.  **081.01.01.01.02** -- Define "mass", "force", "acceleration", and "weight".
3.  **081.01.01.01.03** -- State and interpret Newton"s three laws of motion.
4.  **081.01.01.01.04** -- Explain air density.
5.  **081.01.01.01.05** -- List the atmospheric properties that effect air
    density.
6.  **081.01.01.01.06** -- Explain how temperature and pressure changes affect
    air density.
7.  **081.01.01.01.07** -- Define "static pressure".
8.  **081.01.01.01.08** -- Define "dynamic pressure".
9.  **081.01.01.01.09** -- State the formula for "dynamic pressure".
10. **081.01.01.01.10** -- Describe dynamic pressure in terms of an indication
    of the energy in the system, and how it is related to indicated airspeed
    (IAS) and air density for a given altitude and speed.
11. **081.01.01.01.11** -- State Bernoulli"s equation for incompressible flow.
12. **081.01.01.01.12** -- Define "total pressure" and explain that the total
    pressure differs in different systems.
13. **081.01.01.01.13** -- Apply Bernoulli"s equation to flow through a venturi
    stream tube for incompressible flow.
14. **081.01.01.01.14** -- Describe how IAS is acquired from the pitot static
    system.
15. **081.01.01.01.15** -- Describe the relationship between density,
    temperature, and pressure for air.
16. **081.01.01.01.16** -- Explain the equation of continuity and its
    application to the flow through a stream tube.
17. **081.01.01.01.17** -- Define "IAS", "CAS", "EAS", and "TAS".
