# Contaminated runways Remark: See 010 09 02 04 and 071 02 13 02

## Learning Objectives

1.  **032.04.01.07.01** -- Define a "contaminated runway", "wet runway", and a
    "dry runway".
2.  **032.04.01.07.02** -- Describe the different types of contamination: wet or
    water patches, frost-covered, dry snow, wet snow, slush, ice, compacted or
    rolled snow, frozen ruts or ridges.
3.  **032.04.01.07.03** -- Intentionally left blank
4.  **032.04.01.07.04** -- Intentionally left blank
5.  **032.04.01.07.05** -- Define the different types of hydroplaning.
6.  **032.04.01.07.06** -- Explain the difference between the two dynamic
    hydroplaning speeds and state which of them is the most limiting for an
    aircraft operating on a wet runway.
7.  **032.04.01.07.07** -- State that some wind limitations may apply in case of
    contaminated runways. Those limitations are to be found in Part B of the
    Operations Manual - Limitations.
8.  **032.04.01.07.08** -- State that the procedures associated with take-off
    and landing on contaminated runways are to be found in Part B of the
    Operations Manual - Normal procedures.
9.  **032.04.01.07.09** -- State that the performance associated with
    contaminated runways is to be found in Part B of the Operations Manual -
    Performance.
