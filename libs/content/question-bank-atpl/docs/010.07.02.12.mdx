# Separation methods and minima

## Learning Objectives

1.  **010.07.02.12.01** -- Explain the general provisions for the separation of
    controlled air traffic.
2.  **010.07.02.12.02** -- Name the different kinds of separation used in
    aviation.
3.  **010.07.02.12.03** -- State the difference between the type of separation
    provided within the various classes of airspace and the various types of
    flight.
4.  **010.07.02.12.04** -- State who is responsible for the avoidance of
    collision with other aircraft when operating in VMC.
5.  **010.07.02.12.05** -- Describe how vertical separation is obtained.
6.  **010.07.02.12.06** -- State the required vertical separation minimum.
7.  **010.07.02.12.07** -- Describe how the cruising levels of aircraft flying
    to the same destination and in the expected approach sequence are correlated
    with each other.
8.  **010.07.02.12.08** -- Name the conditions that must be adhered to when two
    aircraft are cleared to maintain a specified vertical separation between
    them during climb or descent.
9.  **010.07.02.12.09** -- State the two main methods for horizontal separation.
10. **010.07.02.12.10** -- Describe how lateral separation of aircraft at the
    same level may be obtained.
11. **010.07.02.12.11** -- Explain the term "geographical separation".
12. **010.07.02.12.12** -- Describe track separation between aircraft using the
    same navigation aid or method.
13. **010.07.02.12.13** -- Describe the three basic means for the establishment
    of longitudinal separation.
14. **010.07.02.12.14** -- State the minimum standard horizontal radar
    separation in NM.
15. **010.07.02.12.15** -- Describe the method of the Mach number technique.
