# Take-off distances

## Learning Objectives

1.  **034.04.01.01.01** -- Explain the effects of the following variables on the
    flight-path and take-off distances: take-off with HIGE or HOGE; take-off
    procedure; obstacle clearances both laterally and vertically; take-off from
    non-elevated heliports; take-off from elevated heliports or helidecks;
    take-off from a TLOF.
2.  **034.04.01.01.02** -- Explain the effects of the following variables on
    take-off distances: mass; take-off configuration; bleed-air configurations.
3.  **034.04.01.01.03** -- Explain the effects of the following meteorological
    conditions on take-off distances: wind; temperature; pressure altitude.
4.  **034.04.01.01.04** -- Explain the take-off distances for specified
    conditions and configuration for AEO and OEI.
5.  **034.04.01.01.05** -- Explain the effect of obstacles on the take-off
    distance required.
6.  **034.04.01.01.06** -- State the assumed reaction time between engine
    failure and recognition.
7.  **034.04.01.01.07** -- Explain that the flight must be carried out visually
    up to TDP.
