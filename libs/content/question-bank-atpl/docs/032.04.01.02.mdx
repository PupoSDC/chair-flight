# Take-off distances

## Learning Objectives

1.  **032.04.01.02.01** -- Explain the effects of the following runway (RWY)
    variables on take-off distances: RWY slope; RWY surface conditions: dry, wet
    and contaminated; RWY elevation.
2.  **032.04.01.02.02** -- Explain the effects of the following aeroplane
    variables on take-off distance: aeroplane mass; take-off configuration;
    bleed-air configurations.
3.  **032.04.01.02.03** -- Explain the effects of the following meteorological
    variables on take-off distances: wind; temperature; pressure altitude.
4.  **032.04.01.02.04** -- Explain the consequence of errors in rotation
    technique on take-off distance: early and late rotation; too high and too
    low rotation angle; too high and too low rotation rate.
5.  **032.04.01.02.05** -- Compare the take-off distance for specified
    conditions and configuration for all engines operating and
    one-engine-inoperative.
6.  **032.04.01.02.06** -- Explain the effect of using clearway on the
    field-length-limited take-off mass.
7.  **032.04.01.02.07** -- Explain the influence of aeroplane mass, air density
    and flap settings on V1, V2 and V2MIN and thereby on take-off distance.
8.  **032.04.01.02.08** -- Explain the effect of an error in V1 on the resulting
    one-engine-out take-off distance.
