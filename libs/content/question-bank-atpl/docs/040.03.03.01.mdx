# Decision-making concepts

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.03.03.01.01** -- Define the terms "deciding" and "decision-making".
2.  **040.03.03.01.02** -- Describe the major factors on which decision-making
    should be based during the course of a flight.
3.  **040.03.03.01.03** -- Describe the main human attributes with regard to
    decision-making.
4.  **040.03.03.01.04** -- Discuss the nature of bias and its influence on the
    decision-making process.
5.  **040.03.03.01.05** -- Describe the main error sources and limits in an
    individual"s decision-making mechanism.
6.  **040.03.03.01.06** -- State the factors upon which an individual"s risk
    assessment is based.
7.  **040.03.03.01.07** -- Explain the relationship between risk assessment,
    commitment and pressure of time in decision-making strategies.
8.  **040.03.03.01.08** -- Explain the risks associated with dispersion or
    channelised attention during the application of procedures requiring a high
    workload within a short time frame (e.g. a go-around).
9.  **040.03.03.01.09** -- Describe the positive and negative influences exerted
    by other group members on an individual"s decision-making process (risky
    shift).
10. **040.03.03.01.10** -- Explain the general idea behind the creation of a
    model for decision-making based upon: definition of the aim; collection of
    information; risk assessment; development of options; evaluation of options;
    decision; implementation; consequences; review and feedback.
