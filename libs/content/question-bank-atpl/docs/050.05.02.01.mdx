# Types of precipitation, relationship with cloud types

## Learning Objectives

1.  **050.05.02.01.01** -- List and describe the types of precipitation given in
    the aerodrome forecast (TAF) and METAR codes (drizzle, rain, snow, snow
    grains, ice pellets, hail, small hail, snow pellets, ice crystals, freezing
    drizzle, freezing rain).
2.  **050.05.02.01.02** -- State the ICAO/WMO approximate diameters for cloud,
    drizzle and rain drops.
3.  **050.05.02.01.03** -- State that, because of their size, hail stones can
    cause significant damage to aircraft.
4.  **050.05.02.01.04** -- Explain the mechanism for the formation of freezing
    precipitation.
5.  **050.05.02.01.05** -- Describe the weather conditions that give rise to
    freezing precipitation.
6.  **050.05.02.01.06** -- Distinguish between the types of precipitation
    generated in convective and stratiform clouds.
7.  **050.05.02.01.07** -- Assign typical precipitation types and intensities to
    different cloud types.
8.  **050.05.02.01.08** -- Explain the relationship between moisture content and
    visibility during different types of winter precipitation (e.g. large vs
    small snowflakes).
