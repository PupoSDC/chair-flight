# Adiabatic processes, stability of the atmosphere

## Learning Objectives

1.  **050.03.03.01.01** -- Describe the adiabatic process in an unsaturated
    rising or descending air particle.
2.  **050.03.03.01.02** -- Explain the variation of temperature of an
    unsaturated rising or descending air particle.
3.  **050.03.03.01.03** -- Explain the variation of humidity of an unsaturated
    rising or descending air particle.
4.  **050.03.03.01.04** -- Describe the adiabatic process in a saturated rising
    or descending air particle.
5.  **050.03.03.01.05** -- Explain the variation of temperature of a saturated
    air particle with changing altitude.
6.  **050.03.03.01.06** -- Explain the static stability of the atmosphere using
    the actual temperature curve with reference to the adiabatic lapse rates.
7.  **050.03.03.01.07** -- Define qualitatively and quantitatively the terms
    "stable", "conditionally unstable", "unstable" and "indifferent".
8.  **050.03.03.01.08** -- Illustrate with a schematic sketch the formation of
    Foehn.
9.  **050.03.03.01.09** -- Explain the effect of the advection of air (warm or
    cold) on the stability of the air. Remark: Dry adiabatic lapse rate = 1
    degrees C/100 m or 3 degrees C/1 000 ft; average value at lower levels for
    saturated adiabatic lapse rate = 0.6 degrees C/100 m or 1.8 degrees C/1 000
    ft (values to be used in examinations).
