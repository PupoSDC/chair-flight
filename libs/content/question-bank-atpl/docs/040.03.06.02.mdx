# Stress

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.03.06.02.01** -- Explain the term "stress" and why stress is a natural
    human reaction.
2.  **040.03.06.02.02** -- State that the physiological response to stress is
    generated by the "fight or flight" response.
3.  **040.03.06.02.03** -- Describe the function of the autonomic nervous system
    (ANS) in stress response.
4.  **040.03.06.02.04** -- Explain the relationship between arousal and stress.
5.  **040.03.06.02.05** -- State the relationship between stress and
    performance.
6.  **040.03.06.02.06** -- State the basic categories of stressors.
7.  **040.03.06.02.07** -- List and discuss the major environmental sources of
    stress in the flight crew compartment.
8.  **040.03.06.02.08** -- Discuss the concept of "break point" with regard to
    stress, overload and performance.
9.  **040.03.06.02.09** -- Name the principal causes of domestic stress.
10. **040.03.06.02.10** -- State that the stress experienced as a result of
    particular demands varies among individuals.
11. **040.03.06.02.11** -- Explain the factors that lead to differences in the
    levels of stress experienced by individuals.
12. **040.03.06.02.12** -- List the factors that influence the tolerance of
    stressors.
13. **040.03.06.02.13** -- State that stress is a result of perceived demands
    and perceived ability.
14. **040.03.06.02.14** -- Explain the relationship between stress and anxiety.
15. **040.03.06.02.15** -- Describe the effects of anxiety on human performance.
16. **040.03.06.02.16** -- State the general effect of acute stress on people.
17. **040.03.06.02.17** -- Describe the relationship between stress, arousal and
    vigilance.
18. **040.03.06.02.18** -- State the general effect of chronic stress and the
    biological reaction by means of the three stages of the general adaptation
    syndrome (Selye): alarm, resistance, and exhaustion.
19. **040.03.06.02.19** -- Explain the differences between psychological,
    psychosomatic and somatic stress reactions.
20. **040.03.06.02.20** -- Name the typical common physiological and
    psychological symptoms of human overload.
21. **040.03.06.02.21** -- Describe the effects of stress on human behaviour.
22. **040.03.06.02.22** -- Explain how stress is cumulative and how stress from
    one situation can be transferred to a different situation.
23. **040.03.06.02.23** -- Explain how successful completion of a stressful task
    will reduce the amount of stress experienced when a similar situation arises
    in the future.
24. **040.03.06.02.24** -- Describe the effect of human underload/overload on
    effectiveness in the flight crew compartment.
25. **040.03.06.02.25** -- List sources and symptoms of human underload.
