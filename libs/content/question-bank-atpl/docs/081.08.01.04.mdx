# Straight, steady glide

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.08.01.04.01** -- Describe the forces that act on an aeroplane in a
    straight, steady glide.
2.  **081.08.01.04.02** -- Name the forces parallel and perpendicular to the
    direction of flight. Apply the formula for forces parallel to the direction
    of flight (D equals W sin gamma). Apply the formula for forces perpendicular
    to the direction of flight (L equals W cos gamma).
3.  **081.08.01.04.03** -- Describe the relationship between the glide gradient
    and the lift-drag ratio, and calculate glide range given: initial height;
    L-D ratio; glide speed and wind speed.
4.  **081.08.01.04.04** -- Define VMD (speed for minimum drag) and explain the
    relationship between alpha, VMD and the best lift-drag ratio.
5.  **081.08.01.04.05** -- Explain the effect of wind component on glide angle,
    duration, and distance.
6.  **081.08.01.04.06** -- Explain the effect of mass change on glide angle,
    duration, and distance, given that the aeroplane remains at either the same
    airspeed or at VMD.
7.  **081.08.01.04.07** -- Explain the effect of configuration change on glide
    angle and duration.
8.  **081.08.01.04.08** -- Describe the relation between TAS, gradient of
    descent, and rate of descent.
9.  **081.08.01.04.09** -- Define VMP (speed for minimum power) and describe
    that the minimum rate of descent in the glide will be at VMP, and explain
    the relationship of this speed to the optimum speed for minimum glide angle.
10. **081.08.01.04.10** -- Discuss when a pilot could elect to fly for minimum
    glide rate of descent or minimum glide angle, and why speed stability or
    headwinds/tailwinds may favour a speed that is faster or slower than the
    optimum airspeed in still air.
