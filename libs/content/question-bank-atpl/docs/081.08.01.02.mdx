# Straight, steady climb

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.08.01.02.01** -- Define "flight-path angle" (gamma).
2.  **081.08.01.02.02** -- Describe the relationship between pitch attitude,
    gamma and alpha for zero-wind and zero-bank conditions.
3.  **081.08.01.02.03** -- Describe the forces that act on an aeroplane in a
    straight, steady climb.
4.  **081.08.01.02.04** -- Name the forces parallel and perpendicular to the
    direction of flight. Apply the formula relating to the parallel forces (T
    equals D plus W sin gamma). Apply the formula relating to the perpendicular
    forces (L equals W cos gamma).
5.  **081.08.01.02.05** -- Explain why thrust is greater than drag.
6.  **081.08.01.02.06** -- Explain why lift is less than weight.
7.  **081.08.01.02.07** -- Explain the formula (for small angles) that gives the
    relationship between gamma, thrust, weight, and lift-drag ratio, and use
    this formula for simple calculations.
8.  **081.08.01.02.08** -- Explain how IAS, alpha, and gamma change in a climb
    performed with constant vertical speed and constant thrust setting.
