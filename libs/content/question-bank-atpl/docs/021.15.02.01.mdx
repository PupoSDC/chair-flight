# Types

## Learning Objectives

1.  **021.15.02.01.01** -- Describe the following tail-rotor systems:
    - delta-3 hinge effect;
    - multi-bladed delta-3 effect;
    - Fenestron or ducted fan tail rotor;
    - no tail rotor (NOTAR) low-velocity air jet flows from tangential slots
      (the Coanda effect);
    - NOTAR high-velocity air jet flows from adjustable nozzles (the Coanda
      effect).
2.  **021.15.02.01.02** -- Identify from a diagram the main structural
    components of the four main types of tail-rotor systems.
3.  **021.15.02.01.03** -- Explain and describe the methods to detect damage and
    cracks on the tail rotor and assembly.
4.  **021.15.02.01.04** -- Explain and describe the structural limitations to
    the respective tail-rotor systems and possible limitations regarding the
    turning rate of the helicopter.
5.  **021.15.02.01.05** -- Explain and describe the following methods that
    helicopter designers use to minimise tail-rotor drift and roll: reducing the
    couple arm (tail rotor on a pylon); offsetting the rotor mast; use of "bias"
    in cyclic control mechanism.
6.  **021.15.02.01.06** -- Explain pitch-input mechanisms.
7.  **021.15.02.01.07** -- Explain the relationship between tail-rotor thrust
    and engine power.
8.  **021.15.02.01.08** -- Describe how the vertical fin on some types reduces
    the power demand of the tail rotor.
