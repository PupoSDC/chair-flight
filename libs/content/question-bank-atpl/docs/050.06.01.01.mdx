# Description, classification and source regions of air masses

## Learning Objectives

1.  **050.06.01.01.01** -- Define the term "air mass".
2.  **050.06.01.01.02** -- Describe the properties of the source regions.
3.  **050.06.01.01.03** -- Summarise the classification of air masses by source
    regions.
4.  **050.06.01.01.04** -- State the classifications of air masses by
    temperature and humidity at source.
5.  **050.06.01.01.05** -- State the characteristic weather in each of the air
    masses.
6.  **050.06.01.01.06** -- Name the three main air masses that affect Europe.
7.  **050.06.01.01.07** -- Classify air masses on a surface weather chart.
    Remark: Names and abbreviations of air masses used in examinations: first
    letter: humidity continental (c) maritime (m) second letter: type of air
    mass arctic (A) polar (P) tropical (T) equatorial (E) third letter:
    temperature cold (c) warm (w)
