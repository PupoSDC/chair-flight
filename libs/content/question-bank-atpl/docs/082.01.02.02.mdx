# Aerodynamic forces on aerofoil elements

## Learning Objectives

1.  **082.01.02.02.01** -- Define the angle of attack (alpha).
2.  **082.01.02.02.02** -- Describe: the resultant force from the pressure
    distribution and the friction at the element; the resultant force from the
    boundary layers and the velocities in the wake; and the loss of momentum due
    to friction forces.
3.  **082.01.02.02.03** -- Resolve the aerodynamic force into the components of
    lift (L) and drag (D).
4.  **082.01.02.02.04** -- Define the lift coefficient (CL) and the drag
    coefficient (CD).
5.  **082.01.02.02.05** -- Show that the CL is a function of the alpha.
6.  **082.01.02.02.06** -- Explain how drag is caused by pressure forces on the
    surfaces of an aerofoil and by friction in the boundary layers. Define the
    term "profile drag".
7.  **082.01.02.02.07** -- Define the L-D ratio.
8.  **082.01.02.02.08** -- Use the lift and drag equations to show the influence
    of speed and density on lift and drag for a given alpha.
9.  **082.01.02.02.09** -- Define the action line of the aerodynamic force and
    the CP.
10. **082.01.02.02.10** -- Know that symmetrical aerofoils have a CP that is
    approximately a quarter chord behind the leading edge.
