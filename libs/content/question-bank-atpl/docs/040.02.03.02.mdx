# Body rhythm and sleep

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.02.03.02.01** -- Name some internal body rhythms and their relevance
    to sleep. Explain that the most important of which is body temperature.
2.  **040.02.03.02.02** -- Explain the term "circadian rhythm".
3.  **040.02.03.02.03** -- State the approximate duration of a "free-running"
    rhythm.
4.  **040.02.03.02.04** -- Explain the significance of the "internal clock" in
    regulating the normal circadian rhythm.
5.  **040.02.03.02.05** -- State the effect of the circadian rhythm of body
    temperature on an individual"s performance standard and on an individual"s
    sleep patterns.
6.  **040.02.03.02.06** -- List and describe the stages of a sleep cycle.
7.  **040.02.03.02.07** -- Differentiate between rapid eye movement (REM) and
    non-REM sleep.
8.  **040.02.03.02.08** -- Explain the function of sleep and describe the
    effects of insufficient sleep on performance.
9.  **040.02.03.02.09** -- Explain the simple calculations for the sleep/wake
    credit/debit situation.
10. **040.02.03.02.10** -- Explain how sleep debit can become cumulative.
11. **040.02.03.02.11** -- State the time formula for the adjustment of body
    rhythms to the new local time scale after crossing time zones.
12. **040.02.03.02.12** -- State the problems caused by circadian disrhythmia
    (jet lag) with regard to an individual"s performance and sleep.
13. **040.02.03.02.13** -- Differentiate between the effects of westbound and
    eastbound travel.
14. **040.02.03.02.14** -- Explain the interactive effects of circadian rhythm
    and vigilance on a pilot"s performance during flight as the duty day
    elapses.
15. **040.02.03.02.15** -- Describe the main effects of lack of sleep on an
    individual"s performance.
16. **040.02.03.02.16** -- List the possible strategies to cope with jet lag.
