# Accelerate-stop distance

## Learning Objectives

1.  **032.04.01.03.01** -- Explain how the accelerate-stop distance is affected
    by given conditions and configuration for all engines operating and
    one-engine-inoperative.
2.  **032.04.01.03.02** -- Explain the effect of using a stopway on the
    field-length-limited take-off mass.
3.  **032.04.01.03.03** -- Explain the effect of an error in V1 on the resulting
    accelerate-stop distance.
4.  **032.04.01.03.04** -- Explain the effect of runway slope or wind component
    on the accelerate-stop distance.
5.  **032.04.01.03.05** -- Explain how the accelerate-stop distance is
    determined and discuss the deceleration procedure.
6.  **032.04.01.03.06** -- Explain how the accelerate-stop distance is affected
    by the use of brakes, anti-skid, reverse thrust, ground spoilers (lift
    dumpers) and by brake energy absorption limits, delayed temperature rise and
    brake temperature indication.
7.  **032.04.01.03.07** -- Explain the hazards of rejecting a take-off from high
    ground speed or high take-off mass, and how to manage these hazards.
