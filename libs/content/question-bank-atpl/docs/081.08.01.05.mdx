# Steady, coordinated turn

## Learning Objectives

1.  **081.08.01.05.01** -- Describe the forces that act on an aeroplane in a
    steady, coordinated turn.
2.  **081.08.01.05.02** -- Resolve the forces that act horizontally and
    vertically during a coordinated turn (tan phi equals V squared over gR).
3.  **081.08.01.05.03** -- Describe the difference between a coordinated and an
    uncoordinated turn, and describe how to correct an uncoordinated turn using
    turn and slip indicator or turn coordinator.
4.  **081.08.01.05.04** -- Explain why the angle of bank is independent of mass,
    and that it only depends on TAS and radius of turn.
5.  **081.08.01.05.05** -- Resolve the forces to show that for a given angle of
    bank the radius of turn is determined solely by airspeed (tan phi equals V
    squared over gR).
6.  **081.08.01.05.06** -- Calculate the turn radius of a steady turn given TAS
    and angle of bank.
7.  **081.08.01.05.07** -- Explain the effects of bank angle on: load factor (LF
    equals 1 over cos phi); alpha; thrust; drag.
8.  **081.08.01.05.08** -- Define "angular velocity".
9.  **081.08.01.05.09** -- Define "rate of turn" and "rate-1 turn".
10. **081.08.01.05.10** -- Explain the influence of TAS on rate of turn at a
    given bank angle.
11. **081.08.01.05.11** -- Calculate the load factor and stall speed in a turn
    given angle of bank and 1g stall speed.
12. **081.08.01.05.12** -- Explain situations in which turn radius is relevant
    for safety, such as maximum speed limits on departure or arrival plates, or
    outbound speed categories on approach plates, and the implications/hazards
    of exceeding given speeds.
13. **081.08.01.05.13** -- Describe the hazards of excessive use of rudder to
    increase the rate of turn in a swept-wing aeroplane.
