# Vision

## Learning Objectives

1.  **040.02.02.03.01** -- Name the most important parts of the eye and the
    pathway to the visual cortex.
2.  **040.02.02.03.02** -- State the basic functions of the parts of the eye.
3.  **040.02.02.03.03** -- Define "accommodation".
4.  **040.02.02.03.04** -- Distinguish between the functions of the rod and cone
    cells.
5.  **040.02.02.03.05** -- Describe the distribution of rod and cone cells in
    the retina and explain their relevance to vision.
6.  **040.02.02.03.06** -- Explain the terms "visual acuity", "visual field",
    "central vision", "peripheral vision" and "the fovea", and explain their
    function in the process of vision.
7.  **040.02.02.03.07** -- List the factors that may degrade visual acuity and
    the importance of "lookout".
8.  **040.02.02.03.08** -- State the limitations of night vision and the
    different scanning techniques at both night and day.
9.  **040.02.02.03.09** -- State the time necessary for the eye to adapt both to
    bright light and the dark.
10. **040.02.02.03.10** -- State the effect of hypoxia, smoking and altitude in
    excess of 5 000 ft on night vision.
11. **040.02.02.03.11** -- Explain the nature of colour blindness.
12. **040.02.02.03.12** -- Distinguish between monocular and binocular vision.
13. **040.02.02.03.13** -- Explain the basis of depth perception and its
    relevance to flight performance.
14. **040.02.02.03.14** -- List the possible monocular cues for depth
    perception.
15. **040.02.02.03.15** -- State that for high-energy blue light and UV rays,
    sunglasses can prevent damage to the retina.
16. **040.02.02.03.16** -- Explain long-sightedness, short-sightedness and
    astigmatism.
17. **040.02.02.03.17** -- List the causes of and the precautions that may be
    taken to reduce the probability of vision loss due to: presbyopia; cataract;
    glaucoma.
18. **040.02.02.03.18** -- List the types of sunglasses that could cause
    perceptional problems in flight.
19. **040.02.02.03.19** -- List the measures that may be taken to protect
    oneself from flash blindness.
20. **040.02.02.03.20** -- State the possible problems associated with contact
    lenses.
21. **040.02.02.03.21** -- State the current rules/regulations governing the
    wearing of corrective spectacles and contact lenses when operating as a
    pilot.
22. **040.02.02.03.22** -- Explain the significance of the "blind spot" on the
    retina in detecting other traffic in flight.
